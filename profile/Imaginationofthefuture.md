# Imagination of the Future

Some work solves today's problems.

Some work tries to imagine what becomes possible if we solve enough of them.

This page is for the second kind.

It is where I collect long-range thinking about AI, human memory, personalised systems, education, operating systems, space, and the kinds of futures that may look unrealistic now but become practical once tools, compute, and human coordination improve.

## Why this exists

I do not only want to build software for immediate use.

I also want to build toward a future where:

- technology does not make ordinary people feel small
- every computer can become understandable
- assistants become deeply personal, useful, and aligned with human goals
- students can ask larger questions and still stay rooted in where they come from
- remote places are not excluded from the future
- imagination is treated as a serious engineering input

## 2028–2030 direction

One idea I keep returning to is this:

**Every Windows user — especially in India and other developing regions — should have their own deeply helpful assistant.**

Not just a chatbot.

A real support layer.

Something that can help a grandmother pay a bill, help a student stay organised, help a small business owner operate online, and help a person who feels "bad at computers" become capable without fear.

I have used the word **Halo** for that idea.

In that world:

- the assistant works locally when possible
- privacy is respected
- offline support matters
- custom skills can be shared
- the computer becomes less of a machine and more of a cooperative layer

## Longer-range imagination

Some of my thinking goes much further out.

Not because I think it is all imminent, but because imagination changes what we decide to build now.

I keep thinking about futures where:

- hyper-personalised AI becomes an extension of human memory
- students work on real unsolved problems, not just static assignments
- teachers become validators, synthesizers, and guides of curiosity
- local knowledge is preserved before it disappears
- a person's values, voice, and hard-won lessons can remain useful across generations
- villages participate in frontier technology instead of only consuming its outputs
- space, science, and first-principles thinking are part of ordinary life

## Education in that future

A future I care about is one where AI does more of the repetitive work, and humans spend more time on:

- curiosity
- direction
- values
- beauty
- judgment
- collaboration
- asking better questions

In that world, a student from a small village should still be able to investigate chemistry, water systems, astronomy, biology, or engineering with serious tools — without losing their cultural grounding.

## What stays human

Even in the most advanced version of the future, I do not think the point is to remove humans from meaning.

The point is to remove unnecessary friction so humans can do more of what only humans can really do:

- care
- wonder
- choose
- remember
- love
- take responsibility
- imagine
